Sudden Cardiac Arrest Can Happen Anywhere

AEDs save lives when used early. AEDCare exists to educate communities and empower bystanders to act with confidence.

What Is an AED?

An Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is a portable medical device that analyzes heart rhythms and delivers a shock if needed to restore a normal heartbeat during sudden cardiac arrest.

Why Early AED Use Matters

  • Survival decreases 7–10% for every minute without defibrillation
  • Most cardiac arrests occur outside hospitals
  • Early AED access can double or triple survival rates

Our Mission

AEDCare is dedicated to increasing public awareness, ensuring AED readiness, and making life-saving knowledge accessible to everyone.

Awareness Turns Bystanders Into Lifesavers

Education, accessibility, and confidence are the strongest tools against sudden cardiac arrest.
